what i learned

Building rad.typo revealed unexpected insights. Streaming platforms optimize for infinite discovery, but that creates attention scarcity. A limited server means intentional listening, deeper engagement, and fewer skipped tracks.

And when the sun sets, you appreciate that instant availability has shaped our habits deeply. There's something poetic‚Äîand deeply human‚Äîabout music that follows the rhythms of sunlight and weather.

why this matters

This isn't a call to end streaming. Most people just want music that works instantly‚Äîand that's fair.

But rad.typo asks: what is the true cost of digital convenience? What if we designed digital infrastructure to acknowledge constraints instead of hiding them? Could intentional scarcity lead to richer experiences?

We might not need infinite music libraries‚Äîjust better relationships with the music we already have.
