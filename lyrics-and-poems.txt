rewilding
(poem, 2026-01-14)
----------------------------------------
we spread out along the riverbank,
strangers with sandwiches and good boots.

"she drove off the side of the glenshane pass.
no other car was involved" —
an incongruous opener
for a picnic by the river.

his sister had studied microbiology
before their accident.
i nodded,
relaxing into his story,
like descending
into a barrel of ice water.

he spoke of hannibal's elephants,
carthage over the himalayas.
(our tapenade and crackers,
had been vacuum-packed
in an industrial unit in pennyburn.)

later we climbed knockery,
paused at caterpillars,
moths in chrysalis.

his corpuscular retelling
gave him salve,
some solace.
his sister sounded remarkable.

then a woman spoke of her exhibition
in a presbyterian museum —
about the last camera factory in ireland.

another man talked of his skin cancer,
excised under local anaesthetic;
halfway through
the numbness faded,
and he described
the sawing motion,
like tearing
a plastic shopping bag.

we passed remnants
of the american naval tenancy,
nissan huts gone,
concrete paths
driven through rhododendrons —
enough once
to make a colonial lady
complain to the major.
those tanks, in time,
ended up
on normandy beaches.

on our way home in banbridge
i bought my son
an underage cider.
a family of six squealed
through a carvery
beneath crayons and ketchup.

he said he would rewild the barren escarpment
of the mournes,
smoky scots pine,
rain from far away,
     falling diagonally
          against the palimpsest
               of the isle of man
                    and sugarloaf.

archetypal tarot
(poem, 2025-12-10)
----------------------------------------
at this kings cross restaurant
the mint sauce is of questionable origin—
grey, perfunctory.
i have definitely eaten worse.

an infinite chocolate fountain cascades
before a badly keystoned bollywood film
projected on the restaurant's screenless white wall.
busboys in tight, crisply laundered shirts
laugh conspiratorially,
folding napkins,
enjoying the slagging.

a couple younger than us
eat greedily by the window.
these are not their first pints of cobra,
happy in a childless pre-christmas bliss.

a middle-aged woman reads conspicuously to my left.
i suspect a religious text,
given the book's form factor.
her tiny clutch strap bisecting her belly,
much as i have mine,
we patiently await our orders.

a birthday party tips out from downstairs.
a wired, tip-hunting maître d' stalks them to the door.
i can tell he's in charge
by his green roll-neck
and darting, calculating eyes.

we were just like them in our twenties,
eating overpriced sunday lunches
in bucolic, pastiche-colonial bars
near parliament fields.

the projected singer in rose-tinted glasses
gesticulates behind a chorus line,
pointing like a 90s east coast rapper with bleached tips,
his backup group pushing the air wildly
to a reedy horn solo.

the woman to my left
flips her book excitedly to take a phone call,
struggling to hit accept,
the pad of her finger eclipsed by her acrylics.
she sets down her book,
fanning the spine:
archetypal tarot.
